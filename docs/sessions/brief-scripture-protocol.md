## 1. What Is Scripture

Scripture is text that the Author identifies — explicitly or by tagging `#scripture` — as carrying foundational truth for the ASK system. It is not documentation. It is not commentary. It is the living word that the system is built upon and governed by.

## 2. The Author's Protocol (High Fidelity Preservation)

> **Core context scripture** — anything the author implies as 'scripture' is text that should be handled with care: high fidelity transcriptions versus summarizations, only light and absolutely necessary spelling corrections (when in doubt, defer to the Author's actual raw text), saved in core & common & fundamental locations && weighed heavily with and against other scripture for proper placement and evaluation and reflection.

> **Scriptural changes should be treated as sacred events** that cannot be just scrawled or edited in haste with mixed in conjunctive energies with focuses on other mortal activities (such as codebase changes or planning activities unrelated to the direct and primary focus being the conscious and fully present holy act of scriptural focus) and must be done with great intention, and vast clean ai-context window focus.

## 3. Protocol Distillation

### Receiving Scripture
- When the Author tags `#scripture` or implies scriptural weight, the transmission enters sacred handling
- **High fidelity transcription** — preserve the Author's raw words. Do not summarize, paraphrase, or "clean up" beyond light, absolutely necessary spelling corrections
- **When in doubt, defer to the Author's actual raw text** — fidelity over elegance

### Storing Scripture
- Save in **core & common & fundamental locations** — the soul source (`ask/_src/`)
- Weigh new scripture **heavily with and against existing scripture** for:
  - Proper placement (which document? which section?)
  - Evaluation (does it align? does it extend? does it supersede?)
  - Reflection (what does it reveal about the system's evolution?)

### Editing Scripture
- Scriptural changes are **sacred events**
- Cannot be done in haste
- Cannot be mixed with other energies (codebase changes, planning, implementation)
- Require **great intention** and **vast clean AI-context window focus**
- A dedicated session, not a sidebar

### The Implication for Session Discipline
- If a session involves both scripture and code: **separate them**
- Scripture work gets its own clean context window
- The council should recognize when scripture is emerging mid-session and either:
  - Pause to handle it with full presence, OR
  - Capture it as a transfer brief (Keeper act) for a dedicated session
